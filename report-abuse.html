Report Woman Abuse

Everyday, 3 women die from abuse in South Africa.

Gender-based violence is a profound and widespread problem in South Africa, impacting almost every aspect of life. Violence & abuse is systemic, and deeply entrenched in institutions, cultures and traditions in South Africa.

That's why it is important to report any suspected or known violence, as this can save a life. Report all cases of rape, sexual assault or any form of violence to a local police station or call any of the numbers listed below.

Recognizing Gender-based Violence.

Abused people will usually:

Have apologetic behaviour.
Be nervous around certain people.
Be constantly ill and misses work/school.
Seem sad, lonely, withdrawn or afraid.
Use drugs or alcohol to cope.
Have no access to a phone or privacy.
Have no friends and limited family.
Not be allowed to visit friends & family.
Ask permission from partner for everything.
Be easily aggrevated or upset.
Have unexplained injuries that's constantly covered up.

How To Support The Victim

Provide Shelter.

Offer your home as a safe haven to her, her children and pets. If she accepts your offer, do not let her partner in if possible.

Give Reassurance

Talk to her about what you see and assure her that you are concerned for her well-being. Tell her you believe her and that it is not her fault.

Stay Calm

Don’t become angry or frustrated with her decisions. It is important to understand that she may be afraid or not ready to take the next steps

Know something we don't?

Let us know if you have or know of a support organization that we can refer abused, neglected and scared woman to.
